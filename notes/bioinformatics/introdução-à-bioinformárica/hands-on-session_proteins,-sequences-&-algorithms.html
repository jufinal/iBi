CAUTION NOTE

This hands-on session is part of a procedure that will continued at a later session.
Students are invited to keep an individual record for each one of the sequences being analyzed.
Students are strongly encouraged to rely on text-only record, both for input, and output.
This focus the student in the real nature of the raw tool output, and allows for a more flexible exploitation of results.
For alternative approaches, note-taking or spreadsheet software are recommended.

Suggested Tools

  • ExPASy::ProtParam
  • ExPASy::PeptideCutter
  • Phobius (SBC)
  • DeepTMHMM (TUD)
  • PSIPRED (UCL)
  • COILS (PRABI)

Procedure

  1. Store locally the supplied sequences (folder, file and format)
  2. Compare both sequences visually and record the main differences between both sequences
  3. Determine to the best of your abilities
    1. Molecular mass
    2. Isoelectric point
  4. Imagine that you are keen on design a discriminant test for both proteins.
    1. Elect a tool to explore cutting agents that may cut both proteins
      1. No more than twice
      2. One agent that may yield fragments with length in line with the proportions
        1. 1 - 3 - 6
      3. The same agent must cut(?) the other protein in a rather different way
  5. Being a membrane bound protein or not (globular) is rather relevant
    1. Look for signs of transmembrane regions for each protein
      1. Use several tools
      2. Look for concordance between their results
    2. Map them to the sequence
  6. Search for support evidence for any putative transmembrane region
    1. Look int the inferred secondary structure
  7. Add evidence for any hits on the existence of quaternary structure
    1. Coiled-coils regions
    2. Disordered regions with binding potential
