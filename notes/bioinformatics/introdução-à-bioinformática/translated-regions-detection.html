Relevant Definitions

  • Gene gene
    • Definition

      “A unit of DNA which performs one function. Usually, this is equated with the production of one RNA or one protein. A gene contains coding regions, introns, untranslated regions and control regions.”
      Source: EBI::2can
      .

    • Includes
      • Coding regions
      • Introns
      • Non-translated regions
      • Regulatory regions
  • Coding Sequence (CDS)
    • Definition

      “The portion of a gene or an mRNA which actually codes for a protein. Introns are not coding sequences; nor are the 5' or 3' untranslated regions (or the flanking regions, for that matter - they are not even transcribed into mRNA).”
      Source: EBI::2can
      .

    • Limitations
      • Sequence based
      • Introns excluded
      • Only protein-coding genes


    • .

  • Open Reading Frame (ORF)
    • Definition

      “A sequence of successive nucleotide triplets that are read as CODONS specifying AMINO ACIDS and begin with an INITIATOR CODON and end with a stop codon ( CODON, TERMINATOR).”
      Source: NCBI(http://www.nlm.nih.gov/cgi/mesh/2009/ ; UniqueID: D016366)
      .

    • Limitations
      • Codon-dependent reading
      • Similar to CDS, but usually used as reading interpretation.
  • Expressed Sequence Tag (EST)
    • Definition

      “A small sequence from an expressed gene that can be amplified by PCR. ESTs act as physical markers for cloning and full length sequencing of the cDNAs of expressed genes. Typically identified by purifying mRNAs, converting to cDNAs, and then sequencing a portion of the cDNAs. .”
      Source: EBI::2can
      .

Ab initio Methods

  • Orfing
    • Mapping Start and Stop Codons.
      • Using a suitable Genetic Code
      • For each available Reading Frame
        • 3 per Chain

    • Stretches of sequence without a Stop codon, and beginning with a Start codon
      • Putative ORF
    • The longer the putative ORF
      • The more plausible its existence will be
        • Small ORFs
          • Even real
            • Will not be considered.
    • Typical success rate
      • < 85% of the real ORFs present
        • Too low to be really useful.
    • Limitations
      • No introns may be present
    • Example Implementation
      • NCBI::ORF Finder


        • .

  • Matching to Previously Trained Hidden Markov Model (HMM)
    • Probabilistic model that relates tangible states (observable) to hidden ones conveying a feature (e.g. being a gene)
