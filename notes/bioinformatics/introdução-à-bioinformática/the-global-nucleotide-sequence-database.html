The Race for a Database

  • ENA, European Nucleotide Archive

    • Formerly the EMBL Bank
    • Officially born at Heidelberg in 1980
    • Sited at the servers of the EBI (Hinxton) since 1995
  • GenBank

    • Creation declared at Los Alamos National Laboratory in 1982
      • Probably it was created earlier
      • Since 1992 it is sited in the servers of the NCBI (Baltimore)
  • DDBJ

    • Exists since 1986 and is kept by the National Institute of Genetics, NIG, Japan
    • Main receiver of sequence submissions from Japanese laboratories

Enters the Era of Globalization

  • The International Nucleotide Sequence Database Collaboration, INSDC

    • The consortium was created c. 1990 by
      • ENA (EBI)
      • GenBank (NCBI)
      • DDBJ (NIG)


      • .

    • A common data structure mirrored across the servers of the consortium
      • A common primary-key
        • Accession Code, AC
          • <alphanumeric identifier>.<version number>
      • Indexation tags based on MeSH terms
      • Data synchronization across the partner servers
      • Common annotation rules
        • Defined by INSDC directives
          • Namely the FEATURES section
            • The main annotation container
    • Involved repositories


      .

    • A distant cause to the GeneInfo identifier (GI) phase out
      • GI was a transversal primary-key to any entry present in the NCBI databases


      • .
        -It is still available for backward compatibility purposes.
