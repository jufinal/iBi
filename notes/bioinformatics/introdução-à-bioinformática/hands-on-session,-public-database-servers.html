Procedure

  1. Go to the EBI site at the WWW.
  2. Search for the accession code AH005568 and establish what kind of sequence is it (e.g. gene). Store it as a plain-text format (EMBL) file .
  3. Locate the same entry on the NCBI servers
    1. View and store the entry in plain-text (GenBank format)
    2. Open the graphic visualization for the annotated sequence.
  4. Compare, side-by-side, detect and reflect on the differences between them.
  5. Open, and read carefully the ENA tutorial on advanced searches
    1. Take notes on the most important points
  6. Head for UniProtKB main page.
    1. Look for the Protein Spotlight section
    2. Browse the current issue
    3. Do look for a back issue ("Articles"), e.g. select on from the month of your own birthday.
      1. Locate the involved entries Accession Code
      2. Assess the annotation Division from those entries
    4. Open the entry with the Identifier APG2_ARATH
      1. Look for the gene name & synonyms
      2. What is the degree of evidente attributed to this entry?
      3. What is the source taxon?
      4. Access the genomic neighborhood through the NCBI facilities
        1. Register the location of the gene
        2. What are the immediate neighbors, upstream and downstream.
      5. Look for the functional annotation for this gene product, use the InterPro for that effect
      6. Query the UniProt::Swiss-Prot for entries sharing the first InterPro annotation of this protein.
